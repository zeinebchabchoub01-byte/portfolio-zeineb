Case Studies

Doman Innovation Studio — Founder Experience & Ecosystem Map

At the University of Utah’s David Eccles School of Business, Zeineb helped bring clarity to a complex innovation ecosystem where funders, trustees, faculty, and founders lacked a shared map of “who does what, when.”

  • Ecosystem strategy tool mapping 100+ programs across 30+ on-campus organizations.
  • Impact framework and KPI dashboard enabling donor-ready, data-driven reporting.
  • Structured founder journey across 9 cohorts/year (~30+ founders) with clear touchpoints.

Results: Faster founder intake and navigation, clearer handoffs, and better touchpoint completion.

Digital Transformation Center, GIZ Tunisia

Over three years, Zeineb built programs around one thesis: startups are drivers of the digital economy. She designed support across three pillars: access to finance, markets, and expertise.

  • Access to Finance – Flywheel: Multi-instrument national innovation fund (AIR, AIR², DEAL, SAIL) supporting startups and SSOs.
  • Access to Markets – STRIDE & Open Innovation: Regional expansion program (Tunisia → Africa) and OI tracks (Scan & Match, Link4INN, Start’N’Trade) leading to concrete PoCs and market entries.
  • Access to Expertise – CoworkUp & Go Rise: Scaling SSO capacity and designing mentoring journeys for 20+ founders with international mentors.

Selected outcomes: national fund operations covering 100+ startups and 20+ incubators, 60 founders trained for African expansion, and 30 SSOs supported with stronger tools and QA standards.

Alien Dimension — AR EdTech (Founder)

As Co-Founder and Chief Projects Officer of Alien Dimension, Zeineb brought augmented reality into real classrooms and university settings, working at the intersection of pedagogy and technology.

  • Raised €150K pre-seed and led a 6-person software + marketing team.
  • Built classroom-ready AR modules piloted with universities and curriculum bodies.
  • Structured sprints, KPIs, and delivery milestones, implementing Agile practices.

Lessons learned (her To-Don’t List): Validate the payer (not just the user), be ruthless about burn and scope, write cofounder agreements early, and protect founder energy.
