Selected Projects

University of Utah

Doman Innovation Studio

My role. Research Associate / Project Lead

Challenge. Founder support at the University of Utah was rich but fragmented—programs, labs, mentors, and funding existed across campus, yet there was no shared, high-level view to help founders (or operators) navigate what support existed, when, and how.

What we built. The U Venture Ecosystem Map: a strategy and navigation tool mapping 100+ programs across 30+ university entities, informed by 30+ interviews with program leads, faculty, and ecosystem operators.

Results. Clearer founder pathways and faster navigation across campus; improved cross-team alignment around founder outcomes rather than program ownership; and a shared institutional understanding of how founder support actually operates day to day—turning referrals into coordinated, ecosystem-level support.

Read more: U of U Venture Ecosystem Map

GIZ Tunisia

Digital Transformation Center — GIZ Tunisia

Startup Ecosystem Partner (started as Startup Expert). Over three years, my work followed a simple thesis: startups are engines of the digital economy—but only if capital, markets, and expertise move together.
Overall impact: 2,000+ innovators and 30+ startup support organizations.

Access to Finance — Flywheel (National Innovation Fund)
https://startup.gov.tn/en/startup_ecosystem/flywheel

Role. Project Manager (GIZ) & Jury Member · Stakeholders: GIZ · World Bank · CDC (Caisse des Dépôts et Consignations)

Challenge. Founders faced a persistent valley of death: limited friends-and-family capital, weak POC funding, and under-resourced incubators struggling to deliver sustained value.

What we built. A multi-instrument national innovation fund with clear governance, selection and diligence, disbursement, and KPI monitoring.

Instruments.

  • AIR: POC development grants for startups
  • AIR²: Investment-readiness grants
  • DEAL: Program-launch grants for SSOs (up to 200k TND)
  • SAIL: Continuity subsidies for high-performing SSOs

Results. Strengthened early-stage financing pathways and improved ecosystem delivery through sustained funding and KPI-driven monitoring.

Access to Markets — Local & International

STRIDE — Regional Expansion Program (Tunisia → Africa)
https://westerwelle-foundation.com/stride/

Role. Program Designer & Co-Lead · 14 months · €330k

Challenge. African markets were geographically close but operationally hard to enter for Tunisian founders.

What we built. A full-funnel expansion pathway: 60 founders trained → 15 expansion-ready cases → 5 concrete market entries, leveraging Westerwelle Foundation hubs in Tunis, Kigali, Mombasa, and Arusha, and integrating Slush’D Tunisia.

Results. Market entries, partner MoUs, and an open-source Market Entry Toolkit.

Open Innovation Initiatives — From Matching to PoCs

Role. Program Lead / Co-Designer

Challenge. B2B startups struggled to break into corporates and SMEs due to slow procurement and unclear entry points.

What we built. Structured pathways from discovery to PoCs: corporate problem briefs → startup sourcing and matching → PoC scoping → procurement pathways.

  • Scan & Match (with EY)
  • Link4INN (sector-based open innovation)
  • Start’N’Trade (with CEPEX & Ministry of Commerce)

Results. 5+ PoCs delivered with exporters, manufacturers, and corporates.
